Ghana Public Holidays 2026

Official statutory public holidays declared for the Republic of Ghana in 2026.

New Year's Day

Thursday, 1 January 2026
Celebration of the start of the new calendar year.
Public Holiday

Constitution Day

Friday, 9 January 2026
Commemorates Ghana's 1993 Constitution coming into force. Moved from 7 Jan.
Public Holiday

Independence Day

Friday, 6 March 2026
Ghana's independence from British rule declared on 6 March 1957 by Kwame Nkrumah.
National Day

Eid ul-Fitr

Friday, 20 March 2026
End of Ramadan — the Islamic holy month of fasting.
Public Holiday

Shaqq Day Holiday

Monday, 23 March 2026
Public holiday observed the day after Eid al-Fitr.
Public Holiday

Good Friday

Friday, 3 April 2026
Christian commemoration of the crucifixion of Jesus Christ.
Public Holiday

Easter Monday

Monday, 6 April 2026
Celebration of the Resurrection of Jesus Christ — the most important Christian holiday.
Public Holiday

Workers' Day

Friday, 1 May 2026
International Workers' Day, honouring the contributions of the labour movement.
Public Holiday

African Union Day

Monday, 25 May 2026
Commemorative Day celebrating African unity. Observed but not a full public holiday.
Commemorative

Eid al-Adha

Thursday, 28 May 2026
The Feast of Sacrifice — most important feast in the Muslim calendar, commemorating Ibrahim's devotion to Allah.
Public Holiday

Republic Day

Wednesday, 1 July 2026
Marks 1 July 1960 when Kwame Nkrumah proclaimed Ghana a republic.
Public Holiday

Founders' Day

Tuesday, 4 August 2026
Honours the Big Six and other founders who led Ghana's struggle for independence.
Public Holiday

Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Day

Monday, 21 September 2026
Honours the birthday of Osagyefo Dr. Kwame Nkrumah, Ghana's first President and pan-Africanist leader.
Public Holiday

Farmer's Day

Friday, 4 December 2026
Recognises the contributions of farmers and fishermen to Ghana's economy. Observed on the first Friday of December.
Public Holiday

Christmas Day

Friday, 25 December 2026
Christian celebration of the birth of Jesus Christ.
Public Holiday

Boxing Day

Saturday, 26 December 2026
The day after Christmas, traditionally associated with giving to the less fortunate.
Public Holiday
